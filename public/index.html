What is a backtest? The system simulates every trade it would have taken over the last 3 years. This tells us whether the strategy has a real historical edge — not just luck.

How to Read Results

Win Rate — What % of trades were profitable. 50%+ is good for swing trading.
Expectancy — Average profit per trade in R units. Above 0 means the strategy makes money over time. Above 0.2R is solid.
Profit Factor — Gross wins ÷ gross losses. Above 1.5 is strong. Above 2.0 is excellent.
Max Drawdown — The worst peak-to-trough loss. Smaller is better.
What is the optimizer? It tests hundreds of combinations of settings (like minimum score, risk/reward ratio, hold time) and finds which combination performed best historically. Always check that sample size is at least 25 trades before trusting results.

What is Walk-Forward Testing? A regular backtest can "cheat" by fitting to history. Walk-forward testing splits the data: it trains on older data, then tests on newer data the system has never seen. If the strategy works on unseen data, that's much stronger proof it's not just overfitted to the past.

Test Window Results (Folds)

Each "fold" is one train/test cycle. Train window = data the system learned on. Test window = unseen data it was tested on.
